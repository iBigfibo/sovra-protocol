Common Questions

Everything you need to know before depositing.

How does Sovra generate real yield?
90% of your USDC deposit is sent to a regulated custodian who purchases actual sovereign Eurobonds. Coupons are passed onchain as USDC.
Why is my effective APR lower than the bond's headline rate?
Two factors: 10% reserve earns 0%, and 10% yield share to protocol. Example: 10.375% × 0.9 × 0.9 = 8.40% net.
Can I withdraw at any time?
Yes, no lock-ups. Small redemptions from onchain reserve; larger ones trigger bond liquidation. 0.1% exit fee applies.
Do I need to complete KYC?
No. Permissionless. Connect wallet, deposit USDC.
What's the difference between USD Vaults and svTokens?
USD Vaults: dollar Eurobonds, no FX risk. svTokens: local currency bonds with higher nominal yields and FX risk (Phase 2).
What are the fees?
0.1% entry, 0.1% exit, 10% of gross yield as protocol share.

1. Introduction

Sovra Protocol is a decentralized finance (DeFi) infrastructure that bridges the gap between the $30+ trillion global sovereign bond market and onchain capital. For the first time, retail and institutional investors can access high‑yield emerging market (EM) sovereign bonds without intermediaries, minimum investment thresholds, or identity verification.

By tokenizing real‑world assets (RWAs) — specifically USD‑denominated Eurobonds and local currency government bonds — Sovra delivers sustainable, transparent yield sourced directly from government coupon payments. This whitepaper outlines the protocol's design, economic model, risk management, and long‑term vision.

Key Insight

EM sovereign bonds offer yields far above developed market treasuries (e.g., Nigeria ~10%, Egypt ~8%, vs. US T‑bills ~4%). Yet these assets are traditionally inaccessible due to high minimums ($100k+), broker gatekeeping, and complex settlement. Sovra removes these barriers.

2. The Problem

2.1 Inefficient Access to Sovereign Debt

Investing in EM government bonds requires navigating a fragmented, opaque, and costly ecosystem:

  • High Minimums: Institutional lots typically start at $200,000.
  • Broker Intermediation: Multiple layers of fees and slow settlement (T+2 or longer).
  • KYC/AML Friction: Extensive paperwork and jurisdictional restrictions.
  • Liquidity Constraints: Exiting a position can take days and may incur significant bid‑ask spreads.

2.2 The DeFi Yield Gap

Existing onchain yield sources are either volatile (LP farming), unsustainable (token emissions), or offer low returns (T‑bill protocols). There is a clear demand for stable, real‑world yield that is both higher than TradFi equivalents and uncorrelated with crypto market cycles.

3. The Sovra Solution

Sovra aggregates user deposits and, through a regulated custodian partner, purchases actual sovereign bonds. The bond's coupon payments are streamed back onchain as USDC yield to depositors. The protocol is non‑custodial at the user level — you always retain control of your funds via smart contract vaults.

“Deposit USDC. Earn real bond yield. No broker. No KYC. No minimum.”

Sovra offers two product lines:

  • USD Eurobond Vaults: Backed by USD‑denominated EM sovereign bonds. No foreign exchange (FX) risk. Yields range from 5.5% to 10.4% gross.
  • svTokens (Phase 2): Local currency bond vaults with higher nominal yields (15–40%) but explicit FX exposure. These are DeFi‑composable ERC‑20 tokens.

4. Architecture

The protocol operates through three coordinated layers:

4.1 Flow of Funds

  1. User deposits USDC into a selected vault.
  2. 90% of deposit is sent to the custodian to purchase the target bond.
  3. 10% remains in the vault's onchain reserve.
  4. Bond coupons are paid to the custodian, converted to USDC, and deposited back into the vault contract.
  5. Yield is distributed to depositors according to their chosen frequency.

5. Vault Mechanics

5.1 Deposit and Representation

When you deposit USDC, you receive a non‑transferable vault receipt (until Phase 2 svTokens) representing your pro‑rata share of the underlying bond and reserve. The receipt tracks your principal and accrued yield.

5.2 Redemptions

Redemptions are always possible:

  • Small Redemptions (≤ onchain reserve): Instant — USDC is sent directly from the reserve to your wallet.
  • Large Redemptions ( > reserve): The custodian liquidates a portion of the bond and returns USDC to the vault. This may take 1–3 business days (TradFi settlement).

Sovra employs a simple, transparent fee model:

No hidden fees. The 10% yield share covers all operational costs, including custodian fees, gas costs, and protocol development. The net APR displayed in the app already accounts for both the reserve drag and the yield share.

7. Risk Framework

While Sovra is designed to minimize risk, users should understand the following:

7.1 Sovereign Credit Risk

The underlying bonds are issued by emerging market governments. There is a non‑zero chance of default or restructuring. Sovra diversifies across multiple countries to mitigate concentration risk. All bonds are senior unsecured USD Eurobonds, which historically have high recovery rates.

7.2 Custodian Risk

The regulated custodian holds assets in a segregated account. In the unlikely event of custodian insolvency, the bonds remain the property of the vault (users). Sovra will partner with multiple custodians over time for redundancy.

7.3 Smart Contract Risk

All Sovra smart contracts undergo rigorous audits by top‑tier firms (reports published). The code is open‑source and immutable where appropriate.

7.4 FX Risk (svTokens only)

Local currency vaults are subject to depreciation of the underlying currency vs. USD. The nominal yield is higher to compensate for this risk. Users should assess their own currency outlook.

7.5 Liquidity Risk

Large redemptions that exceed the onchain reserve may take several days to settle. The 10% reserve is designed to cover >95% of expected daily redemption volume based on historical DeFi vault behavior.
