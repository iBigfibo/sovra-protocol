Risks & transparency

What happens if the country defaults on its Eurobond? +
Sovereign default is a real risk. Sovra diversifies across multiple emerging markets. In a default, users may face delays or partial loss – identical to holding the bond directly.
How do you prevent custodian failure? +
Bonds are held by regulated, licensed custodians with monthly audits and proof-of-reserve attestations. However, custodian insolvency remains an inherent off-chain risk.
Is the smart contract audited? +
Audit with CertiK is scheduled before mainnet. Oracle exploits are mitigated by circuit breakers and multiple data sources.
Why is net APY 8.41% instead of 10%? +
Only 90% of deposits are deployed (10% onchain reserve). After protocol fees, net yield = 8.41% – a trade‑off for instant liquidity.
Can I lose my entire deposit? +
Yes – sovereign default, custodian failure, or smart contract hack could cause loss. We mitigate via audits, reserves, and diversification, but risk remains.
What happens if everyone withdraws at once? +
The 10% reserve covers normal outflows. In a “bank run”, redemptions would be processed pro‑rata from bond sales (1‑2 days). The protocol can pause redemptions to prevent fire‑sales.
