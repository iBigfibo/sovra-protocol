Risks & transparency

What if a country defaults on its Eurobond? +
Sovereign default is a real but historically rare event for countries that issue Eurobonds. Sovra diversifies across six emerging markets (Nigeria, Angola, Turkey, Egypt, Pakistan, Brazil) and will add more. Even in a worst case, holders of defaulted bonds typically recover 50–80% of principal after restructuring – the same as if you held the bond directly. Our reserve and diversified portfolio reduce the impact of a single default.
What if the smart contract is hacked? +
Only 10% of funds are onchain (the reserve). The remaining 90% is held offchain by a regulated custodian. Our smart contracts will be audited by CertiK or Trail of Bits before mainnet. We also maintain a bug bounty and a circuit breaker to pause deposits in case of anomalies. The onchain portion is isolated from the bond custody.
How is Sovra different from a Ponzi or unsustainable yield farm? +
The yield comes from real government bond coupons, not from new depositors or token inflation. Every dollar of yield is earned by the underlying Eurobond held by a licensed custodian. We publish the bond holdings and a daily proof of reserves. There is no native token, no ponzi mechanics, just real assets.
Can I lose my principal? +
Yes. Sovereign default, custodian failure, or a smart contract exploit could lead to partial or total loss. We mitigate by diversifying countries, using regulated custodians with monthly audits, keeping only 10% onchain, and performing multiple code audits. Risk cannot be zero, but it is lower than many DeFi lending protocols.
What happens if everyone withdraws at once? +
The 10% onchain reserve covers normal redemptions instantly. In a 'bank run', redemptions are processed pro rata from bond sales. Because the bonds are liquid Eurobonds, they can be sold within 1–2 days. A queue system prevents fire sales and ensures fairness. No one gets locked out permanently.
How do I know the real bonds exist? +
We publish a daily proof of reserves that includes a hash of the custodian's bond holdings and a signed attestation. An independent auditor reviews the reserves monthly. The bond ISINs are public and can be verified on the London Stock Exchange or Bloomberg. You don't have to trust us – you can verify the offchain assets yourself.
